Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings (2021)

Shang-Chi, a skilled martial artist, confronts his past after being drawn into the mysterious Ten Rings organization led by his powerful father. The film explores legacy, identity, and family with rich Eastern mythology. Marvel's first Asian-led superhero film was praised for its stunning fight choreography, cultural representation, and emotional depth. Simu Liu’s charismatic performance, combined with grounded storytelling and mystical elements, gave the MCU a fresh and global appeal.

Avengers: Endgame (2019)

After the devastating events of Infinity War, the Avengers unite for a final mission to undo Thanos’ destruction and bring balance back to the universe—culminating in an epic time-travel showdown. A monumental conclusion to the 22-film Infinity Saga, Endgame broke box office records and was lauded for its emotional payoff, massive scale, and fan service done right. The film masterfully balances character arcs, humor, and a powerful farewell to key heroes.

Spider-Man: No Way Home (2021)

After his identity is exposed, Peter Parker turns to Doctor Strange for help, only to shatter the multiverse and face villains from across dimensions. He must learn what it truly means to be Spider-Man. An instant fan favorite, No Way Home delivers nostalgia, emotion, and crossover magic by uniting three generations of Spider-Men. Audiences and critics alike praised it for its heartfelt themes, surprise appearances, and redefining Peter Parker’s journey.
