Places

The Colosseum – Rome, Italy

A symbol of ancient Rome, the Colosseum is a massive amphitheater once used for gladiator battles and public spectacles. It stands as a monument to Roman engineering and history.

Statue of Liberty – New York, USA

Gifted by France, the Statue of Liberty stands tall as a universal symbol of freedom and democracy, welcoming millions of immigrants to the U.S. shores through New York Harbor.

Taj Mahal – Agra, India

Built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, the Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum admired for its architectural beauty and romantic story

Machu Picchu – Peru

Hidden high in the Andes Mountains, Machu Picchu is a well-preserved Incan city offering stunning views and mysterious ruins that fascinate historians and travelers alike.

Burj Khalifa – Dubai, UAE

Standing as the tallest building in the world, the Burj Khalifa offers a modern architectural marvel with observation decks that provide breathtaking views of Dubai’s skyline.

Eiffel Tower – Paris, France

A global icon of France, the Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower offering spectacular panoramic views of Paris, especially enchanting at night with its sparkling lights.
