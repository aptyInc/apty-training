Manali

  • Visiting Manali had been on my bucket list for years, and I finally made the trip — and it was nothing short of magical. Surrounded by towering pine trees and snow-capped peaks, the fresh mountain air felt like a warm hug after months of city life.
  • Beware of the climate there.It is too cold out there than we expected.
Manali Tips
  • Carry thermals and waterproof jackets.
  • Avoid Rohtang in heavy snowfall unless guided.

Ooty

  • My recent escape to Ooty was everything I hoped for and more. Located in the Nilgiri Hills of Tamil Nadu, Ooty greeted me with cool breezes, lush tea gardens, and that sweet smell of eucalyptus in the air. After months of hustle, this peaceful hill station felt like a dream.
  • Pack appropriately for varying temperatures, be mindful of driving conditions on hilly terrain, and respect the local environment by avoiding plastic and littering
Ooty Travel Notes
  • Try the toy train ride to Coonoor.
  • Visit the botanical gardens early in the morning.

Lakshwadeep Islands

  • After years of exploring mountains and cities, I finally decided to chase waves — and Lakshadweep was the perfect escape. Located in the Arabian Sea, these coral islands offer some of India’s most serene and untouched beaches.
  • Indian citizens need an entry permit issued by the Lakshadweep Administration.
  • Foreign nationals are allowed only on specific islands, with additional permissions via travel agencies.
Lakshadweep Warnings
  • No alcohol on most islands (except Bangaram).
  • Avoid stepping on coral — it's protected.
